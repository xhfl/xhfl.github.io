A Raisin in the Sun

A Raisin in the Sun is a story about an average African American family that is affected by racial descrimination and prejudice due to the widespread stereotyping of race, class, and gender throughout the United States in the mid 1900s. The Essential Question, "How do stereotypes of one’s race, class, and/or gender influence and individuals goals or aspirations?" is answered using "A Raisin in the Sun" by showing how the Youngers, are discriminated against socially and financially.
